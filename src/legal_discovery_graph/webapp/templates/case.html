Case study

The Project Falcon matter

An eDiscovery investigation is a story decoded from documents. This site is a live, working decode of one fictional matter. Every answer below is a retrieved, cited passage from the corpus, never generated text.

The matter

Meridian Aerospace Systems funds Project Falcon, an avionics upgrade program, and puts the work out to sealed bid as RFP-2023-011. The contract goes to Northgate Supply Solutions over a lower-priced competitor. Months later, accounts payable notices Northgate's invoices climbing, the CFO demands an explanation, and Internal Audit opens engagement IA-2023-19. What the documents reveal from there is the story this corpus holds.

The discovery production is 455 synthetic documents: 276 emails, 71 invoices, 68 memos, 39 meeting notes, and 1 contract. 39 of them are planted evidence that carries the fact pattern; the other 416 are realistic corporate noise the evidence must be found in. Every person, company, amount, email domain, and identifier is fictional, and the corpus is generated deterministically from a fixed seed, so the whole production and every published metric can be reproduced byte for byte.

How to verify the output

Nothing on this site needs to be taken on trust:

  • Every passage links to its source. The citation under each evidence card opens the full stored document, so a passage can always be read in context. That is the same chain a real production would need.
  • Every metric comes from a committed artifact. The Evaluation page renders the JSON files checked into the repository, produced by evaluation scripts that score against gold labels. That includes the unfavorable finding that graph expansion currently lowers whole-corpus recall at depth 10.
  • Everything regenerates from source. bootstrap_data.py rebuilds the identical corpus and gold labels, and the evaluate_*.py scripts recompute each artifact. Method and caveats: docs/DATA_AND_EVALUATION.md.
The full story, if you would rather not decode it yourself

Daniel Reyes, Meridian's Director of Procurement, steers the Project Falcon award to Northgate Supply Solutions after private meetings with Northgate's Olivia Tran, where they agree to keep correspondence off company systems. Northgate's bid loses on price to Apex Components but wins anyway, justified on "schedule risk and vendor responsiveness" in an award determination made solely by Reyes. In return, Northgate routes quarterly payments through Crestline Holdings LLC, a shell company whose registered-agent address matches a property associated with Reyes.

The scheme surfaces through the paperwork: Northgate's monthly invoice amounts escalate, Elena Vasquez in accounts payable flags the pattern, and CFO Marcus Webb demands answers. Internal Audit opens engagement IA-2023-19 and finds that invoice INV-1051 was paid twice and that the RFP evaluation file is missing its scoresheets. Meridian issues a litigation hold, engages outside counsel Hartwell & Pace LLP, and places Reyes on paid administrative leave. The corpus ends there, by design. What outside counsel concluded, and whether charges were ever filed, is not in these documents, which is exactly what the refusal step of the tour demonstrates.
