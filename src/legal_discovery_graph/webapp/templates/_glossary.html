vector
This passage was found by semantic search: the question and every stored passage are embedded as vectors, and the closest passages by cosine similarity are returned — matching meaning, not keywords.
graph
This passage was reached through the Neo4j entity graph: entities in the vector hits (people, organizations, projects) were expanded along their stored relationships to documents the semantic search alone missed. Its graph evidence trail lists the exact relations that led here.
cosine
Semantic closeness between the question and the passage, from 0 to 1 (1 = identical meaning). When the best match falls below the calibrated threshold of 0.509, the app refuses to present evidence — the corpus likely does not answer the question (measured on the gold query set; see Evaluation).
fused rank score
The ordering score when vector and graph results are interleaved into one ranked list. It ranks passages against each other and means nothing in isolation — evidence strength is the cosine score plus the trail.
potentially privileged
Rule-based markers were detected — privilege phrases (e.g. “attorney-client privileged”) or correspondence with an outside-counsel email domain. This is a review aid that flags documents for a privilege review; it is not a legal determination, and nothing is withheld.
PII
The passage matches personal-data patterns: Social Security, bank account, or routing numbers. In this synthetic corpus every such value is deliberately invalid (e.g. an SSN area number that is never issued).
passage
The unit of retrieval: a contiguous span of a source document. The citation under each card links to the full document so every passage can be read in context.
