Where a passage came from

BadgeMeaning
vector Found by meaning-based search. The question and every stored passage are compared by cosine similarity, so passages match on what they say, not on shared keywords.
graph Reached through the relationship graph. People, organizations, and projects named in the first matches were followed along their stored relationships to documents the search alone missed. The card's evidence trail lists the exact relations that led there.

Scores

ScoreMeaning
cosine How close the passage is to the question in meaning, from 0 to 1. When the best match scores below the calibrated threshold of 0.509, the corpus most likely does not answer the question and the app says so instead of showing weak matches. The threshold was measured on the gold query set; see the Evaluation page.
fused rank score The ordering score used to merge vector and graph results into one ranked list. It only compares passages against each other and means nothing on its own. Evidence strength is the cosine score plus the trail.

Review flags

FlagMeaning
potentially privileged The text carries privilege markers, such as the phrase "attorney-client privileged" or correspondence with an outside-counsel email address. This is an aid for privilege review, not a legal determination, and nothing is withheld.
PII The passage matches personal-data patterns: Social Security, bank account, or routing numbers. Every such value in this synthetic corpus is deliberately invalid.

Results and citations

TermMeaning
passage The unit of retrieval: a contiguous span of a source document. The citation under each card links to the full document so any passage can be read in context.
no supporting evidence The refusal state. The best match fell below the calibrated cosine threshold, so the app reports that the corpus does not support the question rather than presenting weak matches. A link is offered to view the below-threshold matches anyway.
